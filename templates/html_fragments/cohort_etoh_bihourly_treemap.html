Bihourly Ethanol Intake for Cohort {{ image.cohort.coh_cohort_name }}. This treemap plot represents the per-monkey ethanol intake patterns over the titled stage of the experiment. Each full-height column represents a monkey in the cohort. The sections within these columns represent two hours within a day, starting at the beginning of a day's 22 hour open access experiment and moving up. The color indicates the average maximum bout within each two-hour section, as a percentage of the total intake during each day.

The sizes of these sections indicate the average volume consumed within the two-hour block over the titled stage of the experiment. These sections are sized relative to the other two-hour section of each monkey. This means a monkey who on average drank mostly during the first four hours of the experiment day, the bottom two sections will be larger than the upper nine sections.

The widths of the columns are also relative. A monkey who on average drank more than his/her peers will have a wider column. Control monkeys are not shown on this treemap.
